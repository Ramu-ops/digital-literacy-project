# Cybercrime Case Study – UPI/Online Payment Fraud

## What is UPI Payment Fraud?
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of 
cybercrime where scammers trick users into sending 
money or revealing sensitive payment information 
through deceptive means.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. The scammer contacts the victim via phone, 
   WhatsApp or SMS, pretending to be a bank 
   official, customer support agent or known contact.

2. They inform the victim of a fake problem such as 
   "Your account will be blocked" or "You have won 
   a cashback reward."

3. The scammer sends a fake payment REQUEST link 
   through UPI apps like PhonePe, GPay or Paytm, 
   disguised as a money receipt.

4. The victim, believing they are receiving money, 
   enters their UPI PIN — which actually APPROVES 
   a payment TO the scammer.

5. Money is instantly debited from the victim's 
   account and transferred to the fraudster.

## Who is Usually Targeted?
- First-time UPI users and college students
- Elderly people unfamiliar with digital payments
- People buying/selling on OLX or Facebook 
  Marketplace
- Anyone looking for part-time jobs online

## Consequences
- Immediate financial loss with little chance 
  of recovery
- Psychological stress and loss of trust in 
  digital payments
- Identity theft if personal details were shared
- Legal complications if the victim unknowingly 
  participates in money laundering chains

Based on cybercrime patterns reported by the
